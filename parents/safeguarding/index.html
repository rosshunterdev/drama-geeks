Safeguarding

The safety and welfare of every child in our care is our absolute first priority.

Our Commitment

Drama Geeks is committed to the safeguarding and welfare of all children and young people in our care. We follow the guidelines set out by the NSPCC and comply with all relevant legislation including the Children Act 1989 and 2004.

We believe that the welfare of the child is paramount, and that all children, regardless of age, disability, gender, racial heritage, religious belief, sexual orientation or identity, have a right to equal protection from all types of harm or abuse.

DBS Checks

All Drama Geeks tutors, staff and regular volunteers hold a current Enhanced DBS (Disclosure and Barring Service) certificate. These are renewed at regular intervals.

No adult works with our students in an unsupervised capacity without a current Enhanced DBS certificate in place.

Safeguarding Training

All staff complete safeguarding awareness training, updated annually. Our designated safeguarding lead holds a Level 3 Safeguarding qualification.

Photography & Filming Policy

Drama Geeks takes photographs and video of students during classes and productions for use on our website and social media. We obtain written consent from parents or guardians before publishing any images of children.

Parents are welcome to photograph and film their children during performances but we ask that you do not publish images of other children without their parent's consent.

If you wish to withdraw consent for photography at any time, please contact us at cereid89@icloud.com.

Reporting a Concern

If you have any concern about the welfare of a child at Drama Geeks, please speak to our Designated Safeguarding Lead immediately. If you believe a child is in immediate danger, contact the police on 999.
