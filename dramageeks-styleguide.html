01 / Brand Identity

Brand Voice

Principle 01

Write for the parent, not the child

Our classes are for kids, but our communications are for the adults booking and paying for them. Speak to parents as capable people making an informed decision, not as recipients of a sales pitch.

Principle 02

Warm and confident, not boastful

We know what we are good at, and we say so plainly. We do not inflate our credentials or stack superlatives. Confidence that comes from substance does not need exclamation marks to land.

Principle 03

Specific, not vague

Concrete details build trust faster than grand claims. A real venue name, a class time, an age range: these are worth more than a page of aspirational language. If you cannot be specific, do not write it yet.

Blue: three-place rule

Blue should appear in no more than three places within any single viewport. It reads as an accent and a signal. Used more freely, it stops reading as a signal and starts reading as noise.

Yellow: two appearances per view

Yellow is for emphasis only. Cap it at two visible instances per screen. It must always be paired with Ink text, never Cream. Never use Yellow as a large section background: at scale it overpowers everything else on the page.

04 / Components

Buttons

Primary

Yellow fill, Ink text. Use for the single most important action on a page. One per screen.

Secondary

Blue fill, Cream text. For supporting actions. Keep to two or three per screen alongside a Primary.

Outline

Transparent with Blue border. Fills to Blue on hover. For tertiary actions or where visual weight should be lower.

Ghost

Transparent with Cream border on dark backgrounds (photos, Ink sections). Fills to Cream on hover.

06 / Foundations

Border Radius

Sharp

0px, default

Card

12px, --r-card

Pill

100px, --r-pill

Use Sharp (0px) on structural elements: section dividers, images, tables. Use Card (12px) on contained components: callout boxes, colour swatches, form inputs. Use Pill (100px) exclusively on interactive controls: buttons and tags.
